What is EBITDA and why is it used?

EBITDA - Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortization. EBITDA = Revenue - Expenses (excluding tax, interest, depreciation and amortization). EBITDA can be used to analyze and compare profitability between companies and industries because it eliminates the effects of financing and accounting decisions. It is a non-GAAP metric that allows a greater amount of discretion as to what is (and is not) included in the calculation. Companies can change the items included in their EBITDA calculation. EBITDA is not the same as cash flow.
