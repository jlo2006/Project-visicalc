Revenue

($ in thousands)

ProTip: Revenue can be unpredictable. Try three scenarios - best guess (base case), things go great (upside case), things go terrible (downside case) - to understand, and plan for, a range of outcomes.

Profit (EBITDA)

($ in thousands)

ProTip: EBITDA is a common way to measure a company’s profitability. It is frequently used by investors, lenders and analysts to compare profitability between companies (why?).

Cash flow

($ in thousands)

ProTip: Companies can profitable but still losing money. Cash flow includes financing and investing outflows that can be large but are excluded from profit or net income. Cash flow is the real bottom line. Why cash flow matters

Cash and debt balances

($ in thousands, end of year)

ProTip: Many recommend keeping enough cash on hand to cover at least 3 months of expenses at any time. If your 'downside' case results in a cash balance lower than this, or negative, consider a contingency plan to trim expenses. More tips
